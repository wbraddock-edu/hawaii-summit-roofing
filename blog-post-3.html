Hilo Rainfall and Roof Life Expectancy

📅 April 12, 2025✍ Marcus Kahanamoku⏱ 8 min read

Ask any roofing contractor outside of Hawaii how long a roof should last, and they'll give you the standard answer: 20 to 30 years for asphalt shingles, 40 to 50 for tile, 50 or more for metal. These numbers are based on typical US climate conditions. In Hilo, those numbers don't apply.

Hilo is the wettest large city in the United States. The Hilo weather station regularly records over 140 inches of rain per year — and certain areas on the windward slopes nearby receive over 200 inches annually. This relentless moisture does things to roofing materials that you simply don't see in drier climates.

How Rain Accelerates Roof Deterioration

Constant moisture creates a perfect environment for algae, moss, and lichen growth. On asphalt shingles, algae appears first as black or dark green streaking. Moss growth goes deeper, lifting shingle edges and allowing water to infiltrate beneath them. Lichen — the most damaging — actually roots into the shingle granule layer, pulling granules away when it grows and tears away when it dries out.

The granule layer is the primary UV and water protection on an asphalt shingle. Once it degrades significantly, the underlying asphalt bakes in the sun, becomes brittle, and eventually cracks. In Hilo's combination of constant rain and intense tropical sun between storms, this cycle accelerates dramatically.

Typical Roof Lifespans in Hilo

Based on our experience replacing roofs across the Big Island, here's what we typically see: basic three-tab asphalt shingles last 8–12 years; premium architectural shingles with algae resistance last 15–22 years; concrete tile lasts 25–35 years; clay tile 35–45 years; and metal roofing 40–60+ years. These are real-world observations, not manufacturer claims.

Extending Your Roof's Lifespan

Annual cleaning removes algae and moss before they establish deep roots. Zinc strips installed near the roof ridge release zinc oxide in rain, which flows down and prevents algae growth below. Ensuring adequate attic ventilation reduces moisture buildup that accelerates material deterioration. Regular inspection — ideally each year before hurricane season — catches small issues before they become large repairs.

When to Replace vs. Repair

If your asphalt shingle roof is under 15 years old with isolated damage, repairs generally make financial sense. If it's over 15 years old, has widespread algae or granule loss, has been repaired multiple times, or shows signs of structural deterioration, replacement will likely save you money in the long run.
