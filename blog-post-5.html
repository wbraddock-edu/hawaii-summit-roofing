Metal vs Tile Roofing in Hawaii: Which Is Right for You?

📅 February 18, 2025✍ Marcus Kahanamoku⏱ 8 min read

Two of the most popular premium roofing choices on the Big Island are metal roofing and tile roofing. Both are significant upgrades over standard asphalt shingles, both have strong aesthetic appeal, and both can last for decades when properly installed. But they're very different products with very different characteristics. Here's the complete comparison from our team at Summit Roofing Hawaii.

Metal Roofing: Performance in Hawaii

Standing seam metal roofing is our most-requested premium product for a reason. Aluminum and Galvalume steel panels shed water immediately with no absorption — critical in Hilo's daily rains. Metal is resistant to algae, mold, and lichen (all significant problems for other materials here). It reflects solar heat, which can meaningfully reduce cooling costs in Hawaii's climate. And in hurricanes, properly installed standing seam metal roofing can withstand wind speeds exceeding 160 mph when correctly attached with the right clip system.

Metal roofing costs: $14,000–$35,000 for a typical residential home, depending on size, pitch, and material choice (steel vs. aluminum vs. copper). Expected lifespan: 40–70 years. Maintenance: minimal. Annual cleaning and periodic inspection of fasteners is typically all that's required.

Tile Roofing: Aesthetics and Longevity

Clay and concrete tile roofing has a timeless aesthetic that suits Hawaii's plantation-style and Spanish-inspired homes beautifully. Tile is extremely durable — individual tiles can last 50+ years. Water runs between tiles and off the roof quickly, and tile doesn't absorb or retain moisture the way wood or composite materials do.

The challenges: Tile is heavy. Clay tile can weigh 800–1,000 lbs per square foot, and most homes require structural assessment before installation. Individual tiles can crack from impact — falling branches or someone walking on the roof — and matching replacement tiles can be difficult for older styles. Installation costs run $18,000–$40,000+ for a typical home.

The Decision: Which to Choose?

Choose metal roofing if: you want maximum weather performance, plan to stay in the home long-term, prefer minimal maintenance, or your home has a simpler roof geometry. Metal is our top recommendation for most Hilo homeowners who are making a long-term investment.

Choose tile roofing if: aesthetics are a priority and you love the classic Hawaiian look, your home's structure can support the weight, and you're prepared for occasional individual tile replacement. Tile is an excellent choice for the right home and the right owner.

Both options dramatically outperform asphalt shingles in Hawaii's climate. The best choice is the one that fits your home, your budget, and your plans. We're happy to walk through the specifics with you during a free consultation.
