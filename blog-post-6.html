Roof Inspection Checklist for Hawaii Homeowners

📅 January 9, 2025✍ Marcus Kahanamoku⏱ 8 min read

Regular roof inspections are one of the most cost-effective maintenance habits a Hawaii homeowner can develop. Catching a small problem early — a few lifted shingles, minor flashing separation, a small rust spot on gutters — can prevent thousands of dollars in water damage repair. This checklist gives you a framework for a basic ground-level inspection you can perform yourself, along with guidance on when to call a professional.

Safety First: Stay on the Ground

This inspection checklist is designed to be performed from the ground and from safe vantage points — a ladder placed securely against the house to look at eave edges, windows for attic views, and from a second-story deck or hillside if available. Do not walk on your roof without proper training and equipment. A fall from a residential roof is a serious injury event.

Ground-Level Exterior Check

  • Walk around the entire perimeter of your home and look up at all roof surfaces
  • Look for missing, curling, or visibly damaged shingles
  • Note any areas where the roof sags or dips between rafters
  • Check gutters for separation from fascia, sagging sections, or damage
  • Look for dark streaking (algae) or green growth (moss/lichen) on shingles
  • Examine chimney flashing and any visible pipe boot flashings for gaps or rust
  • Check that ridge cap appears uniform and intact along the roof peak

Gutter Inspection

  • Clean gutters and check for significant granule accumulation (sign of shingle wear)
  • Ensure downspouts extend at least 4 feet from foundation
  • Check gutter hangers — no loose or missing fasteners
  • Look for rust spots, holes, or separated seams in gutter sections

Attic Inspection

  • On a bright day, check for any pinpoints of daylight through the roof deck
  • Look for dark water stains, mold, or soft spots on the decking
  • Check that ridge vents and soffit vents are clear and unobstructed
  • Ensure insulation is dry and not compressed in corners
  • Note any musty smell — this indicates moisture infiltration

When to Call a Professional

If you observe any of the following, contact Summit Roofing immediately: daylight visible through attic deck; multiple missing or damaged shingles; visible sagging sections; active leaks or water stains on interior ceilings; flashing that is visibly separated or heavily corroded; or gutters that are pulling away from the fascia.
