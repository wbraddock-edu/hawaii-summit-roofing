Best Roofing Materials for Hawaii Rain

📅 June 15, 2025✍ Marcus Kahanamoku⏱ 8 min read

Hilo, Hawaii is one of the wettest cities in the entire United States, averaging over 140 inches of rainfall per year. That's nearly four times the national average. For homeowners and property managers on the Big Island, this isn't just a weather statistic — it's a fundamental engineering challenge that starts at the very top of your home.

Choosing the wrong roofing material in Hilo isn't just a cosmetic mistake. It can lead to premature failure, costly water damage, mold growth, and expensive emergency repairs at the worst possible times. At Summit Roofing Hawaii, we've spent two decades evaluating how different materials perform under relentless tropical rain. Here's our honest assessment.

Metal Roofing: The Gold Standard

Standing seam metal roofing is our top recommendation for most Big Island homes. Galvalume steel, aluminum, or copper panels create a virtually seamless barrier against water. There are no individual shingles to lift, no granules to wash away, and no organic material to grow mold and algae. A quality metal roof installed correctly in Hilo will last 40 to 70 years with minimal maintenance.

The downsides: metal roofing costs more upfront — typically $14,000 to $35,000 for an average home — and can be noisy during heavy rain without proper insulation underlayment. But when you calculate cost per year of service, metal roofing often comes out ahead of any other option.

Architectural Asphalt Shingles

Quality architectural shingles remain popular in Hawaii for good reason. They're cost-effective (typically $8,500–$18,000 for a full re-roof), available in many colors, and when properly selected, can perform reasonably well in tropical climates. Look for shingles rated for high wind (110+ mph), with Class 4 impact resistance, and with algae-resistant granules (look for Scotchgard or similar treatments).

The key limitation: asphalt shingles in Hilo's constant moisture and UV exposure typically last 15–25 years, compared to 30+ years in drier climates. Plan on replacing them before the warranty period ends if your home faces north or is heavily shaded.

Tile Roofing

Concrete and clay tile roofing looks beautiful and lasts a very long time — 40 to 50 years in many cases. Tile handles rain well because water flows between and beneath the tiles to the underlayment and off the roof. The challenge in Hawaii is the weight (clay tile can weigh 900 lbs per square), which may require structural reinforcement, and the cost of replacement when individual tiles crack after impact.

What to Avoid in Hilo

Three-tab asphalt shingles without algae resistance will develop black streaking within a few years in Hilo's humid environment. Untreated wood shakes are similarly problematic — they absorb moisture, harbor mold, and have a dramatically shortened lifespan in our climate. If your current roof uses these materials and is more than 10 years old, it's worth getting an inspection.

Our Recommendation

For most Hilo homeowners, we recommend either a metal roof (for maximum longevity and minimum maintenance) or premium architectural shingles with algae resistance (for lower upfront cost with solid performance). The right choice depends on your budget, roof pitch, HOA requirements, and how long you plan to stay in the home. We're always happy to discuss your specific situation during a free estimate visit.
