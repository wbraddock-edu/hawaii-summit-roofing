Hurricane Roof Prep Guide for Hawaii Homeowners

📅 May 28, 2025✍ Marcus Kahanamoku⏱ 8 min read

Hurricane season in Hawaii runs from June 1 through November 30, and the Big Island is no stranger to tropical storm impacts. While major direct hits are relatively rare, even passing storms and nearby systems can produce wind gusts exceeding 60 mph — more than enough to damage or destroy a poorly maintained roof.

The time to prepare your roof is before the storm forms, not when the National Weather Service issues a watch. Here's the comprehensive preparation guide our team provides to every Big Island homeowner.

Start with an Inspection

Walk around your home and look at the roof from ground level. Look for missing or damaged shingles, lifted ridge caps, rust spots on metal roofing, damaged or pulled gutters, and any visible gaps around chimneys, vents, or skylights. Any of these issues becomes a much bigger problem during a hurricane. If you see anything concerning, call us before storm season begins — not during.

Check and Secure Gutters

Gutters that are full of debris or improperly attached can be torn off by high winds, taking fascia boards with them. Clean your gutters, ensure all downspouts are clear, and check that gutter hangers are securely attached. Add additional hanger brackets if gutters feel loose. Consider installing gutter guards to reduce future debris buildup.

Trim Overhanging Trees

This is the most commonly overlooked pre-hurricane task for Hawaii homeowners. Large branches overhanging your roof can become projectiles in high winds. Hire a certified arborist to trim any branches within 10 feet of the roofline. This protects both your roof and your gutters from impact damage.

Inspect Flashing and Seals

Roof flashing around chimneys, vents, skylights, and roof transitions is a primary entry point for water during storms. Check for lifted, cracked, or corroded flashing. Ensure all penetrations are sealed with fresh roofing caulk or appropriate sealants. This is a relatively inexpensive fix that prevents expensive water damage.

Know Your Emergency Plan

Keep our emergency number — (808) 555-0111 — programmed in your phone. We offer 24/7 storm response with emergency tarping services. If your roof is compromised during a storm, getting it tarped quickly is the single most effective way to minimize interior water damage. Have a tarp or two stored in your garage as well for immediate deployment if needed.

Insurance Documentation

Before hurricane season, photograph your roof from multiple angles and store the images in a cloud backup. If you suffer storm damage, these before-photos will be invaluable when working with your insurance adjuster. Summit Roofing has extensive experience creating insurance-ready damage documentation to maximize your claim.
